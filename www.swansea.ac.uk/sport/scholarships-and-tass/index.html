If you're looking to combine university study with high performance sport training, our High Performance Sport and Talented Athlete Sport Scholarship programmes offer a comprehensive support package to help you achieve both your academic and your sporting goals.

Here at Swansea, our experienced team understands the pressures of combining study with the requirements of training for elite-level sport.

We offer athletes on our performance programmes an unrivalled package of support so that they can continue to achieve their sporting dreams whilst excelling academically.

Our comprehensive scholarship programmes incorporate coaching, strength and conditioning training, psychology support and performance lifestyle coaching. Some of our scholarships also offer direct financial support. 

At Sport Swansea, we offer two types of scholarship programme:

High Performance Sport Scholarship

Our High Performance Sport Scholarships aim to support student athletes competing at the highest possible level, who have achieved international or national representation in their chosen sport.

Benefits of a High Performance Scholarship:

  • Free access to the University gyms and swimming pool.
  • Free on-site parking on both Singleton and Bay Campus.
  • Individual strength and conditioning training.
  • Anti-doping education.
  • Individualised sports injury rehabilitation.
  • Performance lifestyle support.
  • Performance analysis access.
  • Sports psychology support.
  • Nutritional support.
  • Fitness testing.
  • Academic flexibility and support provided through the Talented Athlete Scholarship Scheme (TASS).
  • A series of workshops designed to support the demands of balancing a dual career, enhancing performance and supporting employability and future career planning.
  • Financial support (up to £2000).

Talented Athlete Scholarship Scheme (TASS)

Here at Swansea University, we are proud to be leading the way in supporting talented athletes in education in Wales. We were the first Welsh University to be accredited by the Talented Athlete Scholarship Scheme (TASS) and have been awarded TASS Dual Career Accreditation, which recognises our commitment to supporting student athletes to excel in both academia and sport while studying at Swansea.

Dual Career Accreditation represents a unique partnership between support services and academic staff from across the University, and demonstrates our commitment to ensuring dual career pathways are available to student athletes.

We believe that by providing academic flexibility, as well as the correct environment and support, student athletes can thrive and fulfil both their sporting and educational potential.

For more information on TASS, or to find out how to access support, please visit the TASS website or contact TASS by email.

Talented Athlete Scholarship

Our Talented Athlete Scholarship aims to cover the enormous spectrum of talent that young people reveal in individual and team sports.

Benefits of a Talented Athlete Scholarship:

  • Free access to the University gyms and swimming pool.
  • Free on-site parking on both Singleton and Bay campus.
  • Small group strength and conditioning training.
  • Anti-doping education.
  • Sports injury and rehab.
  • Performance lifestyle support.
  • Sports psychology support.
  • A series of workshops designed to support the demands of balancing a dual career, enhancing performance and supporting employability and future career planning.
  • Financial support (up to £1000).

How to Apply for a Scholarship

There is one application form covering both scholarships. Once submitted, your application will be assessed by our selection panel, and if successful, you will be informed about which level of scholarship you will be awarded.

Applications for September 2023 entry/enrolment will open on 1 November 2022, please click the button below to apply from 1st November.

The deadline for applications is March 31st 2023. You will find a list of frequently asked questions below, but if you can't find the response you are looking for, please do email the team. 

Apply From 1st November

Frequently Asked Questions

When can I apply for a sports scholarship?

The application process will open from 1st November and close on the 31st of March 2023. Applications after this date will not be accepted.

Can I apply through clearing?

Our scholarship applications will re-open for one week in August (date TBC) to accommodate those student athletes who are entering through clearing. Applications during this time can be made via our website.

The application deadline has passed, can I still apply?

Unfortunately, you will be unable to apply outside of the application windows. However, as sports scholarships are issued on an annual basis, you would be eligible to apply for the following academic year.

Is there a financial reward?

Our Elite & Performance Scholars will receive up to £2,000 per academic year.
Our Talented Sports Scholars will receive up to £1,000 per academic year.
In addition to the financial benefits, we offer a full range of support services for each scholarship package.

*Please note financial awards denote the maximum award at each level and may vary.

Is there a performance criteria I need to meet?

The scholarship selection criteria for our High Performance sports can be found here: Sports Scholarship Selection Criteria. Applications by athletes not competing in the sports listed will be considered on a case by case basis.   

Should I provide references with my application?

Yes. It is important to provide as much evidence as possible and two named references in support of your application would be beneficial. This can be a letter/supporting statement on headed paper or any supporting evidence such as video clips.

Can I carry my scholarship over to the next academic year if I defer?

You will not be able to carry over your scholarship. You must meet the conditions outlined in your acceptance letter. Should you not, then you will not receive your benefits package for the named year, and you will need to reapply.

When will I receive payment if I am successful?

If you receive a financial award as part of your scholarship, this will be made in two equal instalments: one before the Christmas recess, and one before the Easter recess.

How will I receive my payment if I am successful?

Payment will be made direct to your bank account.

Who makes the final decision on whether a sports scholarship is awarded?

An independent selection panel is made up of performance sport staff, student sport staff and scholarship supervisors.

If I was awarded a scholarship last academic year, do I need to apply again?

Yes. You will need to apply again, as circumstances do change. Please note that holding a scholarship previously does not guarantee you being grated one again.

Can any sport be eligible for the sports scholarship?

Priority is given, but is not limited to, BUCS sports.

Am I able to have money instead of the services offered?

No. If you are successfully awarded a scholarship, you will receive the package applicable to the award granted.
